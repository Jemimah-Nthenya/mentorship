Born a Crime

Book by Trevor Noah

Chapter 1

"Born a Crime" by Travor Noah i a book talking about the complexities of race, identity, and upbringing in apartheid South Africa. In both chapters, Noah recalls his childhood experiences growing up as a mixed-race child in a society where it seemed illegal.Through humor and courage, Travor overcomes the challenges of not belonging to neither the black nor the white communities.This gave him the title "Born a Crime.

The two chapters provide a precise insight into the unfavorable realities of apartheid and the lasting effects on South Africa.Trevor speaks of his relationship with his mother, Patricia, how she goes against the laws by deciding to conceive him, this shows the strength and resilience Patricia had and the love of amother in the diverse society. Trevor speaks about his experience navigating the racial dynamics of South Africa which shed light on the general discrimination and systemicoppression that characterized the apartheid era.

Chapter 2

Trevor's experience in a society full of discrimination, racism and much more, the issue he addresses relates with Kenya and it's history of colonialism and ethnic tension. Just as South Africa, Kenya has battled with the legacy of colonialism, which left lasting scars on its social fabric. The themes of racism, identity and belonging that Travor explores are universal and apply to any society in the world where people are categorized based on their race, ethnicity, or background.

"Born a Crime" is not only a compelling personal story but also a wide story on the human experience and the enduring quest for belonging and acceptance.
